Mexico

Ancient Mayan ruins in Yucatán

Yucatán is home to world-renowned ancient Mayan ruins like Chichén Itzá, famous for El Castillo (Kukulkan Pyramid) and its astronomical precision, alongside significant sites such as Uxmal, known for the unique Pyramid of the Magician, and the jungle-clad Ek Balam, offering well-preserved stucco carvings and climbing opportunities. I was here as a little kid and would like to go again.

Quintana Roo

Quintana Roo is a southeastern Mexican state on the Caribbean coast, famous for tourism with destinations like Cancún, Playa del Carmen, Tulum, and Cozumel, known for stunning beaches, ancient Mayan ruins (Tulum, Cobá), and natural wonders like cenotes and the Sian Ka'an Biosphere Reserve, offering rich history, vibrant culture, adventure, and a developing tourism infrastructure but also facing challenges like cartel activity and hurricane risks.

Mexico City

Mexico City (CDMX) is a vast, high-altitude megalopolis, Mexico's vibrant capital, blending ancient Aztec history (Tenochtitlan) with Spanish colonial architecture and modern skyscrapers, serving as a major cultural, economic, and political hub with diverse neighborhoods, world-class museums, rich cuisine, and challenges like pollution, all set in the scenic Valley of Mexico surrounded by mountains and volcanoes.

Italy

Rome

Rome, the "Eternal City," is Italy's historic capital, a center of Western civilization built on the Tiber River and Seven Hills, famous for its layers of ancient ruins (Colosseum, Forum), Renaissance/Baroque art (Vatican, fountains), and as the heart of the Catholic Church (Vatican City)

Amalfi Coast

The Amalfi Coast is a stunning, cliffside stretch of southern Italy's Campania region, famous for dramatic landscapes where mountains meet the turquoise Tyrrhenian Sea, creating picturesque coves, terraced lemon groves, and colorful, historic towns like Positano, Amalfi, and Ravello. A UNESCO World Heritage site, it's defined by the narrow, winding Amalfi Drive (SS163), offering breathtaking views, beaches, artisan shops, and a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural charm.

Venice

Venice is a unique Italian city built on 118 islands in a lagoon, famous as the "Floating City" with canals instead of streets, connected by over 400 bridges and navigated by gondolas. Known for its stunning architecture, rich history as a maritime power, and art, it features iconic landmarks like St. Mark's Basilica and the Doge's Palace, offering a timeless, car-free environment filled with waterways, historic squares, and intricate back alleys, though it faces challenges like sinking and overtourism.

Switzerland

Swiss National Park

The Swiss National Park (SNP) is Switzerland's oldest national park, a strictly protected Alpine wilderness in Graubünden (Engadin) where nature develops freely, allowing visitors to see wildlife like ibex, deer, and marmots, and rare plants, from 80 km of marked trails within a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, emphasizing observation over interference.

Zermatt

Zermatt is a charming, car-free alpine village in the Swiss Alps, famous for its stunning views of the iconic Matterhorn mountain, offering world-class skiing, hiking, and mountaineering with year-round snow, traditional wooden chalets, electric transport, and a blend of adventure, serenity, and Swiss tradition. It's a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, featuring extensive ski slopes, glacier access, and scenic trails, all centered around the dramatic backdrop of the Matterhorn.

Wengen

Wengen is a charming, car-free alpine village in Switzerland's Jungfrau Region, perched on a sunny terrace high above the Lauterbrunnen Valley, offering stunning views of the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau mountains. Known for its traditional wooden chalets, tranquil atmosphere, and access to world-class skiing (including the Lauberhorn World Cup race) and hiking, it's a picturesque base for exploring the Swiss Alps year-round, accessible only by cogwheel train.
